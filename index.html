The one-sentence summary of this whole theme

Researchers are only human — pushed by confirmation bias, hindsight bias and a “publish-or-perish” incentive structure, they (often unknowingly) turn exploratory fishing into confirmatory claims. The result is a literature that is “too good to be true.” The remedy is to make the line between exploration and confirmation impossible to blur: preregistration, transparency, and replication.
